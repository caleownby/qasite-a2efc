# The Merge of Asynchronous and Synchronous Communication
April 8, 2024

---
This was originally posted on LinkedIn in April of 2024 It has been copied here for ease of reading and preservation.

---

## What is Sync and Async communication?

Sync (Synchronous) communication is when receiving and delivering communication happens in the same space and those involved will do both at the same time. Think: face to face communication.

Async (Asynchronous) communication is when sending and receiving happens at different times. Think: snail mail communication.

When I am speaking with my team, I am sending a message (my voice) to those listening (receiving). At the same time, they are sending a message through their facial expression, body language, and even audible cues (sighs, laughs, groans) to me. When I suggest that we commit to a project in a month, those in the meeting might cross their arms, might look up to the ceiling, or might audibly sigh. These are all signals that allow me to receive their message nearly simultaneously, maybe that 2 weeks is far too short. I can stop sending that message in service to a higher priority message, to clarify what project I am talking about (the smaller one), or seek further information about the effort required (I thought it was very low). This can all happen in a matter of seconds, a very short time to ensure communication is clear.

When communicating through snail-mail, I am unable to receive this speed of communication and therefore unable adjust my message this quickly. However, I am able to send a significant amount of communication to those reading and the question of _when_ I send it and _when_ they receive it is not an issue. I write my mail after breakfast, while you might read your mail before breakfast. We don't need to monopolize your attention during the worst part of your day for me to tell you a lot of stuff that you might forget about anyway- and we don't need to for me to try to communicate before I am fully awake, potentially wasting your and my time.

## Times they are a-changing

With these two examples at the far ends of the spectrum, I find myself moving more towards the middle with _all types_ of communication. Async communication is moving faster through email and text chats. Sync communication is making it more convenient to send significant amounts of communication with less effort through video and voice chats.

In this middle space, we lose the benefits that we originally leveraged for. We operate as if Async; text chats and email, require others to interact when it is convenient for me (when I send the message, or 30 minutes thereafter). Technology limitations prevent us from the full benefits of face to face and Sync; visual feedback is cut as we each have 1/6 of a screen to express ourselves, and audio feedback is cut down due to processing power.

## The sky is not falling

This is not the end of the line of our communication. We can have the best of both worlds but it requires us to be a little more intentional with how we communicate.

It is helpful to make clear and be lenient on our time expectations with text chat and email responses. If you need an immediate response, maybe a call is better, or maybe a specific chat channel is better. If you are constantly expected to respond immediately, train those around you that you only check email 2x a day and messages once an hour.

It can be helpful to use and be aware of the additional signals we can give in video and voice chats. A laughing face emoji tells others that you got the joke, or a shocked face emoji can tell others that you were surprised by the timeline. Text chat supplement can help as well; keeping an eye on the chat as you are sharing can give you insight to how well the team is understanding your points or questions.

Yes, it takes additional effort to do these things, but the ways we have learned our communication patterns are changing. Eventually they will become second nature again.
